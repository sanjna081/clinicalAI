DOCUMENT: Medicare Program Integrity Manual Chapter 3
WHAT IT IS: This document establishes instructions for Medicare contractors including MACs, RACs, CERT, SMRC, and UPICs regarding the verification of billing errors and the deployment of administrative actions. It serves as an operational framework for identifying improper payments, executing data-driven prepayment and postpayment claims reviews, and enforcing compliance through structured corrective strategies to prevent future losses to the Medicare Trust Funds.

WHO QUALIFIES: This manual outlines rules governing Medicare Administrative Contractors, Recovery Audit Contractors, and specialized integrity units during auditing workflows. On the receiving end, all Medicare Fee-for-Service providers, suppliers, and billing entities are subject to these guidelines when their billing patterns exhibit anomalies, high volumes, or significant financial risks that trigger comparative data analysis or random audits.

BILLING AND OPERATIONS: Operational activities dictate that contractors deploy automated edits, non-medical record reviews, and human-led clinical judgment reviews performed by licensed medical professionals. Contractors solicit evidence via Additional Documentation Requests utilizing paper, electronic portals, or the Electronic Submission of Medical Documentation system, enforcing strict turnaround deadlines of thirty or forty-five days. Persistent compliance errors trigger the Targeted Probe and Educate protocol, which mandates sequential review rounds of twenty to forty claims combined with mandatory interactive provider feedback and education sessions.

RELEVANCE TO PAYMENT INTEGRITY: This text is a foundation for payment integrity by explicitly focusing resources on preventing financial vulnerabilities, identifying overpayments, and returning underpayments. It establishes explicit verification criteria for medical necessity, coding accuracy, statutory exclusions, signature authenticity, and appropriate unit-of-service parameters, ensuring that Medicare reimbursements are restricted exclusively to services that strictly conform to established local and national coverage determinations.

RELEVANCE TO CARE MANAGEMENT: For care management, this framework bridges the gap between proper documentation and clinical necessity by validating that patient records, clinical templates, and medical progress notes comprehensively reflect a longitudinal clinical picture. While contractors must refer quality-of-care or condition of participation violations directly to State survey agencies rather than denying claims outright, the manual ensures that treatments, therapies, and durable equipment are delivered safely and justified transparently to protect beneficiary liabilities.
