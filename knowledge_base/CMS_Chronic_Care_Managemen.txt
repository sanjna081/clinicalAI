DOCUMENT: CMS Chronic Care Management (CCM) Services — MLN Booklet MLN909188, June 2025

WHAT IT IS: Chronic Care Management (CCM) is the coordinated management of a patient's two or more chronic conditions expected to last at least 12 months or until death. Services are primarily non-face-to-face and are paid under the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (PFS). CCM focuses on advanced primary care characteristics including 24/7 access, continuous care relationships, preventive care, and prompt sharing of patient health information.

WHO QUALIFIES: Patients with two or more chronic conditions that place them at significant risk of death, acute exacerbation or decompensation, or functional decline. Examples include Alzheimer's disease, arthritis, asthma, atrial fibrillation, autism spectrum disorders, cancer, cardiovascular disease, COPD, depression, diabetes, glaucoma, HIV/AIDS, hypertension, and substance use disorders. Patient cost-sharing applies, though Medigap supplemental coverage may offset this.

WHO CAN BILL: Physicians (MDs and DOs), certified nurse-midwives, clinical nurse specialists, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants. Clinical staff may provide CCM services on an "incident to" basis under general supervision — the billing practitioner does not need to be physically present. Limited-license practitioners such as podiatrists, dentists, and clinical psychologists may not bill CCM but may be consulted or receive referrals.

REQUIRED STEPS BEFORE BILLING: An initiating visit is required for new patients or those not seen within the prior year. It must occur during a comprehensive E/M visit, Annual Wellness Visit, or Initial Preventive Physical Exam, and CCM must be discussed during that visit. The initiating visit is billed separately. HCPCS G0506 may be billed once if the practitioner personally provides extensive assessment and care planning beyond the usual effort. Patient consent — written or verbal — must be obtained and documented before billing begins. Consent is required only once unless the patient switches practitioners.

CARE PLAN AND DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS: Practitioners must maintain a patient-centered electronic comprehensive care plan based on a full physical, mental, cognitive, psychosocial, functional, and environmental assessment. The plan must include a problem list, measurable treatment goals, expected outcomes, symptom management, planned interventions, caregiver assessment, and coordination with outside resources. The care plan must be available promptly within and outside the billing practice, and a copy must be given to patients and caregivers when necessary. Patient demographics, problems, medications, and medication allergies must be recorded in a certified EHR.

ONGOING SERVICE REQUIREMENTS: Practitioners must provide 24/7 patient access to a physician, qualified practitioner, or clinical staff for urgent needs. A designated care team member must be available for routine appointments and regular patient contact. Communication channels must include phone plus at least one asynchronous method such as secure messaging, secure web portal, or email. Care management must include medication review, medication self-management oversight, timely preventive services, psychosocial and functional needs assessment, and coordination with home- and community-based providers.

CCM BILLING CODES: Codes are based on time per calendar month and whether the billing practitioner or clinical staff performs the service. Non-complex CCM billed by clinical staff: 99490 (first 20 minutes), +99439 (each additional 20 minutes). Non-complex CCM billed by the physician or qualified health professional personally: 99491 (first 30 minutes), +99437 (each additional 30 minutes). Complex CCM billed by clinical staff: 99487 (first 60 minutes), +99489 (each additional 30 minutes). Complex CCM requires moderate- to high-complexity medical decision-making. Chronic pain management billed by a physician or qualified health professional: G3002 (first 30 minutes minimum), +G3003 (each additional 15 minutes minimum).

TIME COUNTING RULES: For codes 99487, 99489, 99490, and 99439, time spent by either the billing practitioner or clinical staff counts toward the threshold. Billing practitioner time may count here only if it is not also used to report 99491. For codes 99491 and 99437, only time personally spent by the billing practitioner counts — clinical staff time does not apply.

CONCURRENT BILLING RESTRICTIONS: Non-complex and complex CCM cannot be billed for the same patient in the same calendar month. CCM cannot be billed alongside G0181 (home health supervision), G0182 (hospice supervision), or CPT 90951–90970 (certain ESRD services). Complex CCM and prolonged E/M services cannot be reported in the same calendar month. Time counted toward a CCM code cannot be counted toward any other billed code. CCM codes 99487, 99489, 99490, and 99491 may be reported during a TCM 30-day service period. Only one of RPM or RTM — not both — may be billed concurrently with CCM.

OTHER CARE MANAGEMENT SERVICES — APCM: Advanced Primary Care Management (APCM) is a bundled monthly service combining CCM, TCM, Principal Care Management, interprofessional consultations, and online digital E/M visits. It does not require tracking minutes per month. G0556 is Level 1 for patients with zero or one chronic condition. G0557 is Level 2 for patients with two or more chronic conditions. G0558 is Level 3 for patients with two or more chronic conditions and Qualified Medicare Beneficiary status.

OTHER CARE MANAGEMENT SERVICES — PCM, PIN, CHI, TCM: Principal Care Management (PCM) focuses on a single high-risk chronic condition expected to last at least 3 months; minimum 30 minutes per month is required; codes are 99424, 99425, 99426, and 99427. Principal Illness Navigation (PIN) is performed by auxiliary personnel following an initiating E/M visit for one serious high-risk condition; codes include G0023, G0024, G0140, and G0146. Community Health Integration (CHI) connects patients with unmet social needs to clinical and community resources; codes are G0019 and G0022. Transitional Care Management (TCM) covers the 30-day period after inpatient discharge back to the community; codes are 99495 and 99496, and RHCs and FQHCs may bill CCM and TCM for the same patient during the same period.

RELEVANCE TO PAYMENT INTEGRITY: CCM is frequently miscoded based on incorrect time attribution, improper staff type for the code reported, or concurrent billing violations. Payers should monitor for non-complex and complex CCM billed in the same calendar month, CCM billed alongside G0181, G0182, or ESRD codes, complex CCM reported with prolonged E/M in the same month, and time overlap between CCM and other separately billed codes. APCM codes (G0556–G0558) should not appear alongside the individual CCM codes they bundle.

RELEVANCE TO CARE MANAGEMENT: Patients with two or more chronic conditions — particularly those with high medication burden, frequent ED visits, or recent inpatient stays — are strong CCM candidates. Care management programs should establish workflows to obtain patient consent, assign a designated care team member, and initiate a comprehensive care plan before the first billing month. APCM may be preferable for high-complexity patients where month-to-month needs vary, since it removes the requirement to track clinical minutes.
