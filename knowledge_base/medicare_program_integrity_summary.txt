DOCUMENT: Medicare Program Integrity Manual Chapter 2
WHAT IT IS: This document establishes the guidelines, methodologies, and source systems used by Medicare contractors to analyze claims data. It serves as an essential framework for systematic surveillance, utilizing established statistical methods to identify trends, billing patterns, and data outliers that suggest potential errors, improper payments, or fraudulent activities.

WHO QUALIFIES: This instruction applies specifically to Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs), Unified Program Integrity Contractors (UPICs), and Supplemental Medical Review Contractors (SMRC). Certain introductory portions also govern Recovery Auditors, though they generally follow separate guidelines outlined in their distinct Statements of Work.

BILLING AND OPERATIONS: Operational mandates require contractors to maintain adequate data processing hardware, secure telecommunications with the CMS Data Center, and analytical software. Contractors must employ personnel with clinical expertise, such as registered nurses, combined with skills in programming, statistics, and data mining. Operationally, MACs must review a minimum of 18 to 36 months of data, comparing the current six-month period against the previous six months to establish baseline norms and detect deviations. UPICs must combine claims history from systems like the Integrated Data Repository and National Claims History to store 36 months of data across multiple parts. Primary data sources include the Fraud Prevention System, One Program Integrity, and local claims data, while secondary sources encompass complaints, appeals data, and federal reports.

RELEVANCE TO PAYMENT INTEGRITY: Data analysis serves as the primary tool for identifying high-risk areas, incorrect coding, and non-covered services that pose financial risks to the Medicare program. It guides the implementation of prepayment edit specifications, identifies where Local Coverage Determinations are necessary, and supports the calculation of sampling strategies for overpayment projections. By identifying widespread overutilization of high-volume or high-cost services that might not appear as standard outliers, it actively prevents improper reimbursement schemes.

RELEVANCE TO CARE MANAGEMENT: Although primarily an integrity tool, the manual impacts care oversight by identifying unusual utilization trends and the overutilization of clinical services. It mandates identifying areas requiring targeted provider education and leveraging clinical staff for systematic evaluation. This ensures that clinical delivery aligns with established utilization baselines, helping correct patterns of inappropriate care delivery and informing educational strategies to improve billing compliance across jurisdictions.
